Victor Vector

Under the moniker Victor Vector, Matthew LaBarre uses his platform to advocate for disability rights, accessible housing and transparent governance. Victor Vector is not only an artistic persona but also a legal avatar through which LaBarre represents himself (pro se) in matters that affect his home and community. In 2020 he filed an Article 78 proceeding (case number EFCA20205001843) challenging administrative actions that he believed infringed upon his rights as a disabled homeowner. This page summarises his journey and provides resources for others navigating similar processes.

Background

Article 78 of the New York Civil Practice Law and Rules allows citizens to challenge decisions by state or local agencies. Filing such a petition requires careful documentation and adherence to court rules. Acting without an attorney (pro se), LaBarre prepared his own filings, drawing upon his experience with advocacy and the structured thinking behind his CBARL CSP protocol. Though the specifics of the case are confidential, he emphasises the importance of persistence, clear communication and community support when confronting institutional barriers.

Resources & Notes

  • For information on Article 78 proceedings, consult the New York Unified Court System’s guide which explains how to commence a petition and what remedies are available.
  • To explore LaBarre’s personal testimony and legal documentation, contact him directly via email or follow his updates on social media. He occasionally shares redacted documents and reflections on the process.
  • LaBarre’s legal work is an extension of his healing practice; he views law and sound as complementary means of restoring balance. The ULS observational framework informs his approach by inviting deeper awareness of unseen forces—be they bureaucratic or energetic.
